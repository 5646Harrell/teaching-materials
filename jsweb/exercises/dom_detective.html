Project: DOM Detective

The case of the GirlDevelopIt website

  • Open up girldevelopit.com in Chrome or Firefox, and open up the console.
  • Use DOM access methods to find the following parts of the page:
    • Every image on the page
    • The upper left-hand logo (that says GDI)
    • The logos of the media sources that featured GirlDevelopIt (lifeHacker, TED, etc., at the bottom of the page)
    • The big tagline that says "DON'T BE SHY DEVELOP IT"
    • All of the subtitles that are paragraphs (the subtitles are the light red italicized text in each section)
    • BONUS: all the dots in the map


document.getElementsByTagName("img");
document.getElementsByClassName("logo")[0];
document.getElementsByClassName("press-logos");
document.querySelector(".opener h1");
document.querySelectorAll("p.subtitle");
document.querySelectorAll("circle");

The case of the BrainPickings blog

  • Open up brainpickings.com in Chrome or Firefox, and open up the console.
  • Use DOM access methods to find the following parts of the page:
    • The bylines of each blog post
    • The introductory paragraph of each blog post
    • Every image that is inside a blog post
    • Every link inside the left-hand nav bar
    • The Facebook link in the top right social media bar
    • The input box for entering your name for email subscription, on the left side
    • Every link inside the tag cloud on the left side
